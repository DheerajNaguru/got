John Snow

In A Game of Thrones, Jon Snow is introduced as the 14-year-old illegitimate son of Eddard "Ned" Stark, Lord of Winterfell.

Ned treats Jon as much like his other children as propriety and his honor will allow. Still, as somewhat of an outsider.

Night King

The Night King, an being of immence power and malevolence has taken poor Valerion and turned the cream colored dragon into a blue-eyed devil.

The only harbor for the Night's Watch lies in ruins and the army of the dead marches toward Winterfell. It is a grim cliffhanger.

Eddard Stark

Lord Eddard Stark, also known as Ned Stark, was the head of House Stark, the Lord of Winterfell, Lord Paramount and Warden of the North.

He was the older brother of Benjen, Lyanna and the younger brother of Brandon Stark. He is the father of Robb, Sansa, Arya, Bran and Rickon by his wife.

khaleesi

A khaleesi's status varies greatly from khalasar to khalasar. She could be very influential, riding beside her husband as he leads, or she could be worth practically less than the khal's horse.

It is Dothraki tradition that when a khal dies, his surviving khaleesi must retire to join the crones that rule Vaes Dothrak, known as the dosh khaleen.

Khal Drago

Drogo is a khal or warlord of the Dothraki, the famed nomadic horse-lords of the eastern continent beyond the Narrow Sea.

Drogo was very young to have his own khalasar, particularly such a large one. He is a legendary warrior and has never been defeated in battle.

Stannis Baratheon

Stannis Baratheon is a fictional character in the A Song of Ice and Fire series of epic fantasy novels by American author George R. R. Martin, and its television adaptation Game of Thrones.

Stannis Baratheon is the younger brother of King Robert and older brother of Renly.
